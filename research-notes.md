**DAVID'S RESEARCH**

# AI in Government — Pros
- Efficiency & speed: Automates repetitive tasks, processes large amounts of data quickly.
- Transparency: Creates audit trails, detects fraud/corruption.
- Better public services: 24/7 digital assistance, reduced bureaucracy.
- Cost savings: Less manual processing and reduced waste.
- Improved decision-making: Data-driven insights → better policy planning.
- Potential increase in trust: If decisions are fair and transparent.

# AI in Government — Cons
- Accountability gap: Hard to assign responsibility when AI makes decisions.
- Bias & unfair outcomes: AI can reinforce existing discrimination in data.
- Black-box decisions: Lack of transparency or ability to explain decisions.
- Operational risks: Errors, system failures, cybersecurity issues.
- Public distrust: Citizens may not accept AI decision-makers.
- Digital inequality: Not everyone can access or understand AI-based services.
- Regulatory + infrastructure challenges: Requires strong laws, data quality, and skilled staff.

# Why Albania Chose Diella (AI Minister)
- Fight corruption: Procurement in Albania historically had nepotism, bribery, and irregularities.
- Impartiality: AI is seen as “unbribable” and free from personal interests.
- Modernization: Part of e-government reforms to show progress and efficiency.
- EU integration signaling: Demonstrates commitment to transparency and digital governance.
- Existing system success: Diella already worked well on Albania’s e-Albania platform.

# Pros of Appointing Diella
- Reduces human discretion → fewer corruption opportunities.
- Fast and consistent procurement decisions.
- Symbol of innovation: Positions Albania as a digital governance pioneer.
- Potentially more transparent tender processes.

# Cons / Risks of Diella
- Legal legitimacy issues: Constitution doesn’t clearly support a non-human minister.
- Accountability void: If a procurement decision is wrong, who is responsible?
- Bias in procurement algorithms: May unfairly favor or exclude vendors.
- Opaque decision-making: Hard for citizens or companies to appeal AI decisions.
- Cybersecurity vulnerabilities: Manipulation of data → flawed outcomes.
- Possible political “stunt”: Risks being seen as symbolic rather than real reform.
- Public acceptance concerns: Citizens may distrust an AI in political authority.

# Overall Conclusion
AI can improve government transparency, efficiency, and service delivery — but only when used with strong oversight, clear regulations, transparency, and human accountability.Diella is a groundbreaking experiment, but comes with serious risks around legality, fairness, accountability, and public trust.
